Hops: A 1,200-Year Tradition <0.05% of GDP*

The Žatec (Saaz) region has grown hops since at least the 9th century. Today Czechia is the world's 3rd largest hop producer with 4,852 hectares and ~6,500 tons annually—but dwarfed by Germany (~20,600 ha) and USA (~22,500 ha). 82% is the prized Saaz variety. In 2023, Žatec became the first hop landscape inscribed as UNESCO World Heritage.

Why Saaz Hops Are Special

  • Low alpha acid (3-5%) = subtle, refined bitterness
  • High essential oil content = distinctive spicy/floral aroma
  • Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) — first for any hop in EU
  • The Žatec microclimate: drier than Czech average, ideal conditions
  • Emperor Charles IV banned hop cuttings exports under penalty of death (14th century)
  • Used in Pilsner Urquell, Guinness, Stella Artois, and countless others
