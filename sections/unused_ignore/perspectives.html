What Economists Are Saying

Voices from Czech central bankers, economists, and international observers on the state of the economy:

"We did not become an economic miracle of Central Europe, like Poland, or Ireland... No miracle is expected in 2025. But adopting the euro would give us structure and eliminate excuses."

— Tomáš Sedláček, economist and former advisor to President Havel, December 2024

"In a small, open economy like ours, higher interest rates can neither lower European energy prices nor solve the problem of global supply chains."

— Aleš Michl, Governor of the Czech National Bank, June 2024

"In other economies in the region, growth turned out slower by one third (Czech Republic) or even two thirds (Hungary, Romania) against expectations."

— ING Economic Analysis, 2024 CEE Review

"The German economy is paralyzed. Compared to other major European countries, Germany is falling noticeably behind."

— ifo Institute, Spring 2024 (on Czechia's largest trade partner)

"Addressing structural challenges arising from the country's demographic dynamics and the global energy transition will be critical to maintain sustainable growth."

— IMF Executive Board, 2023 Article IV Consultation on Czech Republic

CNB Governor Michl's Record (2022-2024)

  • Took office July 2022 with inflation at 17.5%
  • Achieved inflation target of 2% by early 2024
  • Implemented "strong koruna" policy—currency hit historic highs
  • Cut policy rate by 300 basis points to 4% (by Nov 2024)
  • Named Central Banking Governor of the Year 2024
