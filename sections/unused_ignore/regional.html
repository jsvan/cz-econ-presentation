Why Is Germany Struggling?

According to economists, Germany's crisis stems from:

  • Energy shock: Loss of cheap Russian gas after 2022 sanctions; prices remain above pre-crisis levels
  • Industrial decline: Energy-intensive industries (chemicals, steel) down 17%+ since 2022; BASF cut 2,600 jobs
  • China slowdown: Less demand for German machinery and autos
  • Structural issues: Decarbonization costs, aging workforce, overregulation
  • Bankruptcies: Up 23.5% in 2023 to 18,100 cases

Impact on Czechia: The Czech Ministry of Finance explicitly lists "structural problems and weak economic growth in Germany" as a downside risk to Czech forecasts.

Czech Structural Challenges

The IMF (2024) identified these key issues for Czechia:

  • Demographics: Aging population, pension system deficit widening
  • Green transition: High industrial share means high decarbonization costs
  • Germany dependency: 32% of exports to one country creates vulnerability
  • Wage gap: Still ~40% below German wages despite low unemployment
