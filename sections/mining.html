Mining: From Europe's Richest to Phase-Out ~1% of GDP

Czechia was once Europe's silver heartland—Jáchymov (1516) gave us the word "dollar." Today: ~30M tonnes coal/year, phase-out by 2033. Ranks 4th globally in kaolin. Future: Cínovec holds Europe's largest lithium deposit.

Nevřeň Underground Mine

One of Europe's few underground kaolin mines, operating since 1904 near Plzeň. The deposit sits 40–80m below surface, accessed via tunnels. Underground mining preserves the overlying farmland and produces higher-purity kaolin than surface quarries—prized for ceramics and paper coating.

Kaznějov Surface Quarry

The largest open-pit kaolin operation in the Plzeň basin, extracting ~1M tonnes annually. The distinctive white terraces reveal layers of kaolinized granite. After extraction, the land is progressively reclaimed—some former pits now serve as lakes and nature reserves.

Severočeské Doly — Lignite

Czechia's largest mining company, owned by ČEZ, operating massive open-pit lignite mines in the North Bohemian Basin near Most and Chomutov. Giant bucket-wheel excavators strip away overburden to reach coal seams up to 30m thick. Produces ~20M tonnes annually, feeding nearby power plants. Phase-out by 2033.

OKD — Hard Coal

The last deep hard coal mine in the EU outside Poland, operating in the Ostrava-Karviná basin since 1782. Miners descend 800–1200m underground to extract high-quality bituminous coal. Once employed 100,000+; now ~8,000 workers remain. Final closure expected by 2025.
